Our Founding Fathers:

  • Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulwahhab: A prominent Islamic scholar and theologian, Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulwahhab is best known for his role in the founding of the religious movement often referred to as Wahhabism. His teachings emphasized the importance of a strict adherence to the Quran and Sunnah, and he formed a pivotal alliance with the House of Saud, which played a significant role in the establishment of the First Saudi State.
  • Imam Turki bin Abdullah: As the founder of the Second Saudi State, Imam Turki bin Abdullah was a key figure in the re-establishment of Saudi rule after the fall of the First Saudi State. His leadership was marked by the recapture of Riyadh and the restoration of Saudi authority in the region, setting the stage for the future unification of the Kingdom.
  • King Abdulaziz Al Saud (Ibn Saud): The founder of the modern Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, King Abdulaziz Al Saud, also known as Ibn Saud, was a visionary leader whose conquests and diplomatic skills led to the unification of the disparate regions of the Arabian Peninsula into a single nation. His reign saw the discovery of oil and the beginning of Saudi Arabia's transformation into a global energy powerhouse.

Explore the Regions:

  1. History: Delve into the past of each region, from the ancient trade routes that crossed the Arabian desert to the birthplace of Islam and the unification of the Kingdom.
  2. Food: Savor the flavors of traditional dishes like Kabsa, the national dish, and other regional specialties that reflect the blend of Persian, Turkish, African, and Indian influences.
  3. Dressing: Understand the traditional attire, from the flowing robes and headscarves that are a response to the desert climate to the modern variations that respect cultural norms while embracing contemporary styles.
  4. Antiquities: Marvel at the archaeological treasures, such as the rock art in the Hail Region and the ancient city of Hegra, which testify to the rich cultural heritage of the Kingdom.
